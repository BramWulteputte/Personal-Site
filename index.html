Video Games in Education

Gone are the days when video games were seen as a distraction from education. Today, we are realizing the potential of gaming as a powerful tool for enhancing learning and engagement. Here are some of the benefits of incorporating video games into education:

  • Improves problem-solving skills, spatial reasoning, and cognitive flexibility.
  • Promotes collaboration, communication, and critical thinking.
  • Increases creativity and innovation.
  • Creates a sense of motivation and ownership, leading to increased engagement and self-directed learning.
  • Can be especially powerful for students who may struggle with traditional learning methods.

It's time to embrace the power of gaming in education and unleash the full potential of our students. Are you ready to join the game-based learning revolution? Follow us at Shapescape for more insights and updates on how video games can be used in education.

Grave Danger 2

Last night, Grave Danger 2 hit the virtual shelves of the Minecraft Marketplace. Exactly 1 year after the original, Shapescape brings a sequel to one of its most downloaded games in which we take our Knight's Brigade on a well-earned vacation after their adventures in Grave Danger. Luckily the King's family owns a resort on Skull Island. Nothing ever goes wrong on islands that have names like that, right?

Grave Danger was downloaded 5.5 million times as a part of the 2022 New Year Celebration. Seeing that established player base got us thinking about whether or not we could turn Grave Danger into an IP with some name recognition. This leads us to the creation of Grave Danger 2. This was produced in a very focused period of time in by our whole production team at our office in Hamburg, Germany.
